Sisters' Camelot

Sisters' Camelot is a non-profit organization based in Minneapolis, Minnesota. They gather food that would otherwise be wasted and deliver it too sites where people can access the food for free. They are committed to feeding their community, and my team and I admired their work and decided to reach out to them. Upon connecting, we learned that they wanted to improve their layout and more clearly display information, and make it easy to donate and volunteer.


Research

While conducting our research, we learned of a few problems that were a priority to address. These included a poor layout, unclear display of information, a difficult search to volunteer or donate. We also confirmed these problems with users through testing of the current website. Users of the Sister’s Camelot website struggle to find where they can support the nonprofit by volunteering. How might we improve this site so that users are able to seamlessly navigate it, therefore increasing donations and volunteers?


Wireframe

After our initial research, our team sketched ways to address these issues with a mobile-first design approach. We made space to design a logo for them and place it at the top, cleaned and clarified the navigation, and used columns to arrage the content in a more appealing way.


User Testing

To ensure our design was thourough and solved potential unseen issues, we tested our prototype numerous times with different users. Setting specific tasks for the user to complete, we learned more about what could be improved. For example, we learned that it would be beneficial to position a link to donate somewhere clear on the homepage. Also, we found that a link directly on the home page could increase volunteers to sign up.


High Fidelity

After testing our high-fidelity prototype solution, we were comfortable knowing the initial problems that Sisters' Camelot had were addressed with our design. The new logo catches attention and serves as a link to the homepage. Users easily found the donate and volunteer links. Users found the layout much easier to navigate. Existing content was presented in a way that further emphasized their purpose as an organization.
